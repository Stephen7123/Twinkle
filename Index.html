Beginning Poems Always Christmas Letter

Read this slowly.

There is no urgency here. No expectations either.

I didn’t create this to persuade you, or to hold you in place. I made it because some feelings deserve a quiet space to exist without interruption.

You don’t owe me anything for reading this. Not reassurance. Not closeness. Not answers. Just your presence — even briefly — is enough.

I know you value your independence, your inner world, and the freedom to step back when things feel heavy. This page respects that.

Twinkle

They say find a queen who builds her own throne,
Mine constructed hers from wit and bone.
Her scepter’s a pen, her decree a laugh,
She divides my days into before and half.

The royal edict? A stunning smile.
I’ll serve this sentence for life, and all the while
Whisper to the court of my foolish heart,
“From this glorious rule, I’ll never depart.”

The Atlas of You

You are my favorite geography:
The quiet valley of your collarbone,
The weather-map of your shifting moods,
The warm coastline where I am home.

I am learning, by heart, the topography of your laugh, and I will spend my life charting every path.

Puzzle Box

You are not a missing piece—That implies I was incomplete.
You are the gentle release of a truth I couldn’t meet alone.

Two complex frames, clicking into something new—
A third, quiet picture with two names, an answer we somehow always knew.

No Demands. Just Truth.

I am not asking you to change. I am not asking you to promise anything.

You are free with me. Free to come closer. Free to step back. Free to be exactly who you are.

If one day you choose to stay — I’ll meet you there. If another day you need quiet — I’ll respect that too.

This isn’t a letter meant to pull you in. It’s one meant to leave the door open.

If you ever return to these words, let them gently remind you: you were never too much, and never a burden.

Christmas, Softly

This page asks nothing of you. It’s just warmth, if you want it.

Christmas doesn’t have to be loud to be meaningful. It can be quiet lights, slow mornings, and the comfort of knowing someone thought of you without needing anything in return.

Wherever you are this season — whether surrounded by people or wrapped in solitude — I hope you feel safe and unhurried.

Merry Christmas, Twinkle.
Gently. Always.
